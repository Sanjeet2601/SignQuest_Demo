Welcome to SignQuest

SignQuest is a premier competition at NeurIPS 2026 focused on Indian Sign Language (ISL) processing. With over 63 million Deaf and Hard-of-Hearing individuals in India, there is an urgent need for advanced AI technologies that bridge the communication gap between sign language users and the broader society.

We propose three core tasks, each with two tracks (RGB Video-based and Pose-based), resulting in six tracks in total. This dual-track design allows systematic exploration of the trade-off between rich visual information and compact, privacy-preserving skeletal representations.

The competition builds upon the iSign and CISLR datasets and aims to drive innovation in multimodal learning, spatio-temporal modeling, and low-resource sign language understanding.

Competition Tasks

1. Sign Language Translation (ISL → English)

Generate natural English sentences from continuous ISL videos or pose sequences (gloss-free).

English: "The ban would mean she can't compete in any national or other domestic events"

Tracks: Video-to-Text • Pose-to-Text

Evaluation: SacreBLEU (primary), ROUGE-L, chrF

iSign Dataset → iSign Paper →

2. Word / Gloss Recognition

Classify isolated signs from short video clips or pose sequences into correct word/gloss labels.

Label: National

Tracks: Video-based • Pose-based

Evaluation: Top-1 Accuracy (primary), Top-5 Accuracy

CISLR Dataset → CISLR Paper →

3. Word Presence Prediction

Given a sentence video/pose and a query word, predict whether the word appears in the signed sentence.

Query: National
Sentence Video
Contains: National → Present

Tracks: Video-based • Pose-based

Evaluation: Accuracy, Precision, Recall, F1 Score

iSign Dataset →

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Who can participate?
A: The competition is open to everyone — students, researchers, and professionals worldwide.

Q: Do I need prior experience in Sign Language Processing?
A: No. Beginners are welcome. We will provide a complete starter kit with baselines.

Q: What datasets will be used?
A: The competition builds upon the iSign and CISLR datasets, along with new high-quality test data collected with ISLRTC.

Q: Is there any registration fee?
A: No. Participation is completely free.

Q: Will the datasets be publicly available?
A: Yes. Training data is already public. The full dataset will be released after the competition under CC-BY-NC license.
